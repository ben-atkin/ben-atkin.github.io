Share an example of when you were trying to work productively with others, but there was resistance or tension. Discuss strategies you tried at the time, how effective they were, and your reflections on what other strategies you would try now and why.

Unfortunately I don’t have a lot of great examples to pull from, however one example includes my friends and I playing a new game to us all called “Eco”, it is a cooperative, survival and resource management game that runs in a fully simulated ecosystem on a planet. You start with stone age knowledge and tools and work your way up to modern day technology. The challenge of the game is balancing how quickly and efficiently you advance your technology and the pollution you create by doing so, for the planet you're on much like our own is susceptible becoming uninhabitable for life.

My friends and I discussed a plan, and it was to divide all the tasks at hand and create professionals in certain areas, like masonry, mechanics, farming, etc. We needed people to focus on a certain area of the game to get further faster. But something we didn’t account for is some of these jobs, like the “Mechanic” for instance required a lot of raw materials to be refined into metals and other substances for them to then work with, so they would have to wait for the professional “Miner” to gather the required resources for them to do anything, and then when the materials came it was most of the time as simple as clicking a button and then waiting for the “Mechanic”. So we ended up with people like the “Farmer” and the “Miner” doing a lot more manual labour and spending more time doing gruelling and boring tasks just for the “Mechanic” to have fun watching Youtube videos and creating new tech that no one had seen before, just for them to always call first dibs because they were the one that made it.

So it created tension between the manual labourers and the tech industry, our solution was to keep the idea of focusing in a certain area to still push our tech everyday but to split more of the boring and annoying tasks between all of us. We as a group tried all tasks as best we could for a day and then all decided by majority decision what were considered the worst tasks, then those tasks would be equally distributed to all. Everyone had a part in the simplest foundations and also the most advanced heights we could achieve, and it more or less solved our problems of people doing too much and others doing too little.

Sorry for the incredibly long story, hopefully the story not only made sense to you, but you learn from our mistakes.
