Javascript and its relationship with HTML and CSS

A good analogy to describe their relationship is a cafe. Imagine the building itself stripped bare bones, just the walls and roof, this is the HTML of the cafe. Then the style of the cafe, the types of chairs and tables they use, the cutlery they have, even the layout of the furniture. This is what I would call the CSS of the cafe. Then the actual employees inside the cafe that take orders, cook food, make hot drinks and serve customers are the Javascript.

Explaining "Control Flow" and "Loops"

For starters Control Flow is the order in which a computer executes statements, from top to bottom, left to right. As if you were reading a book, a computer will read code the exact same way.

Then we have Loops, loops are just how they sound, they loop a certain action over and over again until specified otherwise. For example a loop in Javascript is the same as scrubbing a really grubby pan, when you start scrubbing you have the intention of cleaning it, and after each stroke of the brush you look at where it was just covering to see how well you did, and if there is still muck on the pan you go back over it, and you keep scrubbing until it's clean.

Describing the DOM

The DOM or "Document Object Model" can be used to represent all of the content on a webpage, giving it a structure that can make it easier for a developer to find something specific or just give someone a better understanding of what is happening on a webpage.
